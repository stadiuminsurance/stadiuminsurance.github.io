Questions collectors ask us

Straight answers, no insurance runaround.

Doesn't my homeowner's policy already cover my collection?
Rarely the way you'd hope. Most homeowner's policies cap collectibles at a low sub-limit, exclude theft of high-value "valuables," and won't touch shipping losses or anything that happens at a show. We cover the gaps a standard policy leaves wide open.
Am I covered at card shows, conventions, and trade nights?
Yes. Coverage follows your collection — at home, on the table, and on the drive there and back. Carrying six figures out of a show is exactly the moment most policies leave you exposed.
What about shipping — Whatnot, eBay, or sending cards to graders?
Shipping is one of the biggest risks in the hobby and a core part of what we cover. Items lost, stolen, or damaged in transit through your sales and grading submissions can be protected.
Do I need a professional appraisal first?
No appraisal needed to get started. We help you document and value your collection as part of the process — and agreed values mean you're not haggling over what a card was worth after a loss.
Can you cover business inventory, not just a personal collection?
Absolutely. Whether it's a personal collection, dealer inventory, breaker product, or a full card shop, we build coverage around how your collectibles actually move.
How much does it cost?
It depends on your collection's value, what you collect, and how it's stored and moved — but quotes are free, fast, and come with no obligation. Tell us about your collection and a specialist will follow up.
